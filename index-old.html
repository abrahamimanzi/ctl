ABOUT CRYSTAL TELECOM

Crystal Telecom was established and incorporated in 2013, and following a share transfer of 20% of shares held in MTN Rwanda from Crystal Ventures to Crystal Telecom on 29th April 2015, Crystal Telecom currently owns a 20% equity stake in MTN Rwanda. The Company’s sole business is holding and managing this 20% stake in MTN Rwanda for the benefit of its shareholders. The remaining 80% of MTN Rwanda is held by MTN Group Limited – the largest African mobile telecommunications company with operations in 22 countries including Africa and the Middle East.

Crystal Telecom, a wholly owned subsidiary of Crystal Ventures, owns a 20% share of MTN Rwanda as its only investment. The Crystal Telecom IPO is typical of Crystal Venture’s investment strategy of monetising mature holdings with the intention of redeploying capital in support of early-stage enterprises that can make a significant contribution to the Rwandan economy. The Crystal Telecom IPO further reinforces our groups’ motto, Create Wealth, Enrich Lives.


THE CRYSTAL TELECOM IPO IS A SUCCESS

Crystal Telecom is pleased to announce that subsequent to the close of its IPO offer period on June 5th that the IPO has been fully subscribed subject to listing formalities.
Renaissance Capital, acting as a Lead Transaction Advisor and Bank of Kigali, acting as Receiving Bank together with BK Securities acting as the Lead Sponsoring Broker, have confirmed results to the Capital Markets Authority with orders received totaling in excess of 123% of the shares on offer.
Over 2,300 orders were received and reflect strong demand from Rwandan, East African and International Investors alike. Both the institutional and the retail tranches are oversubscribed.
Jack Kayonga, Chairman of Crystal Telecom said “The IPO when all regulatory formalities are successfully concluded will represent another important milestone in the development of the Rwanda Capital Markets. We are delighted by the enthusiastic take up of the IPO both from local and international investors who will now participate in the ongoing success of MTN Rwanda.“

Celestin Rwabukumba, CEO of the Rwanda Stock Exchange (RSE) said ‘As we look forward to welcoming Crystal Telecom’s shareholders onto the RSE, we also encourage other businesses in the private sector, large or small, to engage in the capital markets by either listing their companies or investing & trading in listed securities. Crystal Telecom has set the pace as the first fully private sector company to utilize the capital markets as a means to raise finance. We expect to see growth in our sector over the coming months as other companies seek to do the same.'
The Company is expected to start trading on the Rwanda Stock Exchange no later than 17th July 2015.

    Crystal Telecom, the first local private company to list on the Rwandan Stock Exchange, has appointed Iza Irame as CEO following the departure of Vincent Gatete. Crystal Telecom is a special purpose vehicle that owns 20% of MTN Rwanda.
    Iza Irame is the Chief Corporate Officer of Crystal Ventures Ltd since May 2016. Prior to joining CVL, Iza was Chief Executive Officer of African Alliance Rwanda for four years. During her tenure she led diverse corporate finance transactions in the Rwandan Market and has been instrumental in growing the local Capital Market industry. She was the first chairperson of the Rwanda Stockbrokers Association.
    Iza holds a Business Administration degree specializing in Finance from the Kigali Institute of Science, Technology and Management (KIST). She is an Affiliate of the UK Association of Chartered Certified Accountant (ACCA).
    Iza has more than 10 years senior management experience both in private and public sector. Prior to joining African Alliance, Iza was Group Finance Manager at Horizon Group. She currently serves as a Non-Executive Director on various boards including the Rwanda Stock Exchange, African Alliance Rwanda, Zigama CSS and Rwanda Teaching University Hospitals.

  • CRYSTAL TELECOM LISTING
    Posted on : 16th July 2015

    The Rwanda Stock Exchange (RSE) will on Friday welcome its third domestic listing, Crystal Telecom, which is expected to start secondary market trading following its successful Initial Public Offering (IPO) last month.

    NEWS DETAILS

    The Rwanda Stock Exchange (RSE) will on Thursday welcome its third domestic listing, Crystal Telecom, which is expected to start secondary market trading following its successful Initial Public Offering (IPO) last month.
    In an interview with Business Times yesterday, Celestin Rwabukumba, the RSE chief executive officer, said the company’s listing will be a great addition to the market in terms offering investors diversified products and bringing more liquidity to the exchange.
    “Judging from the IPO, the company’s shares are attractive to investors and will certainly increase trading at the exchange,” he said. Crystal Telecom, which holds 20 per cent of MTN Group telecom subsidiary, MTN Rwanda, was fully sold to the public last month when its parent firm, Crystal Ventures, offered its shares.
    Rwabukumba said he expects the firm’s shares to perform well at the bourse. The firm’s IPO share price was Rwf105.Prior to the listing, there have been six listed firms at the bourse, four of which are Kenyan cross-listed stocks, and two domestic firms - Bank of Kigali (BK) and Bralirwa. BK and Bralirwa have dominated trading at the bourse for years, with shareholders of the cross-listed companies often accused of not willing to trade their shares to local investors, thus hurting the liquidity of the stock market.
